Test cricket is the longest format of the game and is considered the ultimate test of a player's skill and endurance. Played over five days, it features two innings per team and requires strategy, patience, and resilience. Test matches are known for their unpredictability, as games can shift dramatically from one session to the next. The format has a rich history, with iconic rivalries like the Ashes between England and Australia and memorable performances etched in cricket folklore.
